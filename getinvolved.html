Get Involved

Small things you can do to help

Everyone Can Do Something

You don't need to be an expert or have loads of free time. Whether it's five minutes or five hours a week, there's something here for you to do that makes a difference.

Visit a Local Park

This is the easiest one. Just go. Bring a friend, bring your kids, bring your dog. The more we use these spaces, the more reason there is to protect them.

Join a Community Garden

Community gardens take on volunteers with zero experience. You'll learn how to grow food, pick up some gardening skills, and get to know people in your area you wouldn't otherwise meet.

Plant Something

Trees soak up CO2; and support wildlife. Even a small pot of wildflowers on a balcony gives bees and butterflies somewhere to go. Every little bit helps.

Speak Up for Green Space

When local councils are making planning decisions, they listen to people who show up. Go to a community consultation, sign a petition, or just tell your local rep that parks matter to you.

Your Green Space Action Checklist

Small things done regularly add up. Here's a list you can work through to build greener habits into your week:

  • Get at least 2 hours a week in a green space
  • Walk or cycle to the park instead of driving, if you can
  • Plant one tree or shrub this season
  • Leave a bit of your garden for wildflowers
  • Join one community cleanup this year
  • Bring a friend or family member next time you go
  • Check your Green Wellbeing Score once a month to track how you're doing

Tell Us About Your Green Spaces

We'd really like to hear about your relationship with green spaces. How often do you go? What's your local park like? It takes less than 3 minutes and your answers help us understand the picture across Ireland.

Groups Worth Checking Out

These organisations do real work protecting and expanding green spaces in Ireland. Have a look at what they're up to.

  • Trees on the Land - they plant native trees across Ireland every season, north and south.
  • The All-Ireland Pollinator Plan - bringing councils, communities and farmers together to help bees and other pollinators.
  • An Taisce - the National Trust for Ireland, working on everything from heritage protection to green schools.
  • Community Gardens Ireland - a network that supports people running and setting up community growing spaces.
  • Your local biodiversity officer - most county councils have someone whose job is looking after green space and nature projects in your area.
