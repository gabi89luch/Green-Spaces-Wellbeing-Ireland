    How the Score Works

    We take your four answers and weight them to get a score out of 100. Here's roughly how it breaks down.

    Weekly Nature Time (40%)

    This carries the most weight. A study in Scientific Reports found that at least 120 minutes a week in nature is linked to better health and wellbeing. More time means a higher score, up to a sensible limit.

    How Close You Live (25%)

    SDG 11 says people should have a green space within about 300 metres of home. That's roughly a 5-minute walk. The closer you are, the more likely you are to use it regularly.

    How Often You Go (20%)

    Going once a month is better than nothing, but getting out a few times a week is what really makes a difference. We reward frequency more than one-off visits.

    Outdoor Activity (15%)

    Doing something physical outside means you get the benefit of exercise and nature at the same time. Each active day bumps your score up a bit.
